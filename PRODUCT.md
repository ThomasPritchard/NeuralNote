# Product

## Register

product

## Users

NeuralNote is for Obsidian refugees: technically comfortable researchers, students, writers, and heavy information consumers who want to keep an existing local Markdown vault without maintaining a plugin-built capture and filing system. Their core workflow is to capture a source or thought, let NeuralNote organise it, and ask questions across the resulting library with citations they can inspect.

## Product Purpose

NeuralNote turns mixed source material into a clear, queryable, Obsidian-compatible knowledge base. Success means the user can throw material into one place, recover it later through cited chat, and retain ownership of readable Markdown and YAML files on disk. Citation fidelity, full-source retention, and explicit failures take precedence over convenience.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, and quietly capable. The product should feel trustworthy enough for research, focused enough for sustained knowledge work, and honest about what the AI did or could not do.

## Anti-references

- Obsidian setups that expose plugin assembly, templates, macros, and filing mechanics as the user's job.
- Cloud-notebook experiences that obscure where source material lives or what leaves the device.
- Generic AI chat surfaces that hide provenance, overstate certainty, or use decorative activity to imply work that was not verified.
- Dense developer tools that accept clipped controls, unreadable metadata, or keyboard-inaccessible interactions as the price of power.

## Design Principles

- Keep the user's source and citation trail visible and inspectable.
- Make the opinionated workflow feel direct; progressively reveal setup only when a capability needs it.
- Use calm, familiar product affordances and reserve visual emphasis for state, action, and risk.
- Surface failures where they occur, with a next action when the app can safely provide one.
- Preserve the existing dark-indigo workspace vocabulary instead of inventing per-feature styles.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Every flow must support keyboard operation, visible focus, readable contrast, screen-reader labels and status announcements, and reduced-motion preferences. Narrow panes and long user-controlled vault content must reflow or truncate without hiding the full value from assistive technology.
